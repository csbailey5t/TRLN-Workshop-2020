CHESHIRE (Altrincham) BRANCH.

SIXTY-FIFTH ANNUAL REPORT.

To THE SUBSCRIBERS AND FRIENDS.

The Committee has pleasure in presenting the Sixty-ﬁfth Annual
Report together with Statement of Accounts for the year ending
December 315t, 1939. The year’s work has necessarily been somewhat
disturbed. by the outbreak of war, many of the usual autumn activities
“such as stalls at Agricultural Shows, distribution of Horse Merit
Badges, and. the annual Horse Drivers’ Supper—having had to be
cancelled, and other forms of activity organised, chief among them
being the local branch of the A.R.P. for Animals, the nucleus of which
was formed through the joint interest of the local branch of the
R.S.P.C.A. and the local veterinary surgeons. Later, this was
developed into “ N.A.R.P.A.C., Altrincham Branch,” with animal
casualty posts in Altrincham, Bowdon, Sale, and Knutsford, staffed by
voluntary personnel (trained in anti-gas, and the handling and First
Aid treatment of animals), working under the supervision of veterinary
surgeons.

In December Inspector Hindle, of the Macclesﬁeld and South-East
Cheshire Branch R.S.P.C.A., was recalled for service with the Colours,
and Inspector Drake was requested by Headquarters to be responsible
for the inspection work of both branches, to the best of his ability.
This branch has in the past received much practical help from Inspector
Hindle, and very readily agreed for Inspector Drake to give the
Macclesﬁeld branch all the help he could—which arrangement will
presumably be in operation for the duration of the war.

The Committee realises that under present circumstances subscrip-
tions may be more difﬁcult than ever to obtain, and so is endeavouring
to cut down expenses wherever possible and concentrate on the most
vital parts of the work. Cruelty to animals, whether caused by ignor-
ance, carelessness or sheer brutality, does not cease because we are at
war. If the R.S.P.C.A. through lack of funds were compelled to with—
draw its inspectors, numbers of cases of cruelty to animals would not
be detected nor alleviated. Such a state of things is unthinkable and
therefore the work of the inspectors must go on. Money subscribed
here will be used primarily for the maintenance of the Inspector in
this district, and giving something is one thing all can do for those
who can do nothing for themselves.
